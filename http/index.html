Introduction

This page provides a view of the stars from where you are, and a map of all the stars in the sky.

The tabs select one of a number of subpages;

Sky view
This is your window on to the stars in the sky; it can be zoomed, panned and rotated. An individual star can be selected. The view is not affected by the 'time', 'date', and 'location' settings. The equatorial grid and/or azimuthal grid can be overlaid on top of the view (the azimuthal grid is affected by the location and time/date). The top of the sky view has a green line; the left and right edges are blue, and the bottom is red. This window is shown on the sky map.
Sky map
The sky map is *all* of the stars, using a equirectangular cylindrical projection. An 'equatorial' grid can be overlayed on top of the map, showing the equator, the 30th and 60th parallels, and great circles for every 30 degrees of longitude. An 'azimuthal' grid can be overlayed on top of the map, showing the grid as seen by you at your location and time (if the location and time are set to your location and time!). The rectangular 'sky view' The rectangular sky view windows is shown on the sky map; this usually does not appear as a rectangle (unless the field of view is small), but distorted. The top edge of the window is green (as in the sky view window), and the bottom edge of the window is red (etc). Clicking on the map will centre the sky view window on the that location.
